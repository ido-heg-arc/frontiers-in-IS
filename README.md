# Information Systems Frontiers - CUSO Workshop (19th of May 2026) 


## Overview

Since its emergence, research in **Information Systems** (IS) has been marked by a historical questioning of its recognition as a scientific discipline (Baskerville & Myers, 2002). This quest for recognition has led it to explore its fundamental nature and influences (Jeyaraj & Zadeh, 2020; Sarker et al., 2019), as well as to define or redefine its boundaries (Pratt et al., 2012; Tarafdar & Davison, 2018). This workshop will explore the relationships of IS with connected disciplines such as Human-Computer Interaction (Zhang & Li, 2004) and present the diversity of genres. Relevant methods such as bibliometrics (Baudet et al., 2024; Walsh & Renaud, 2017; Walsh et al., 2022; Walsh & Rowe, 2023) and AI-based tools (Wagner et al., 2021) will be presented and used in practice. Perspectives from researchers at different career stages will be shared. The primary aim of this workshop is to support junior researchers in IS and related fields by offering practical guidance and tools they can apply directly to their own work.

## Program

*The program is subject to minor changes.*

**Introduction [9:00 - 10:15]**
* Welcome, brief presentation of organizers, participants and their research.
* Introduction to IS research by organizers.

**Bibliometric Methods in IS Research [10:30 - 12:30]**
* [Alexandre Renaud](https://www.em-normandie.com/annuaire-des-professeurs/alexandre-renaud), Associate Professor at the EM Normandie (France) will present an overview of bibliometric methods and their use in IS and management research. Practical guidance will be provided.

**Lunch [12:30 - 13:30]**


**Understanding AI to Support Literature Reviews and an Introduction of an Ecosystem of Tools for IS Research [13:30 - 15:00]**
* [Isabelle Walsh](https://www.skema.edu/en/faculty-and-research/professors/isabelle-walsh), Emeritus Professor at the SKEMA Business School (France) will present its recent research on AI and literature reviews as well as provide a hands-on activity to conduct a literature review with AI in IS.

**Qualitative Data Analysis of Bibliometric-based Outputs [15:15 - 16:15]**
* [Sébastien Point](https://www.em-strasbourg.com/fr/chercheur/enseignants/point-sebastien), Full Professor at the EM Strasbourg Business School of the University of Strasbourg (France) will present qualitative data analysis methods and techniques that can be used in bibliometric-based studies.

**Group Discussions and Concluding Remarks [16:15 - 17:00]**
* Group discussion on their own research topics as well as discussion with speakers and organizers.
* Knowledge sharing focusing on the use of bibliometric/AI-based methods, techniques, and tools. Difficulties that can arise during the revision process will be exposed by organizers. The goal is to provide practical advices for junior researchers.
